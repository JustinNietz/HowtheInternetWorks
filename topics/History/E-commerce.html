Age of E-Commerce

In the 1990s a new model of commerce emerged, which was based on using the Internet and harnessing the power of individual customers. New businesses were founded like Amazon.com and eBay.com, which operated their retail business fully online. Let us have a look at their operations.

Amazon.com

Jeff Bezos launched Amazon.com in 1995. His idea came from that time when he was working at a Wall Street firm. He observed the tremendous possibilities in using the Internet to sell products. He made a list of possible items to sell, and his final decision was: books. He reasoned that there were millions of book titles out in the market but there was not such a bookstore that could hold all these titles.

eBay.com

Pierre Omidyar thought a marketplace on the Web would be fairer and more accessible than any other existing market. So, he created eBay.com during a long weekend all by himself in 1995; he needed to code mostly three things for the future users of his auction site: how to create an item, a possibility for accessing all items others already created, and a feature to bid on these items.

The Use of the Internet Spreads Rapidly

Just to put into perspective how fast the Internet spread; to reach 50 million users:

  • Radio needed 38 years
  • Television reached this point in 13 years
  • The Internet had the same amounts of users only in 4 years

This explosive growth was driven by two facts. Firstly, we need to mention Moore’s law which says that the power of integrated circuits doubles every 18 months. Thanks to this fact, computers became smaller and less expensive, in a word, more accessible to an average customer. Secondly, according to Metcalf’s law, every new user does not just add a new connection to the network, its effect are much bigger, as it has the potential to connect to all the other users in the network. It means that as the number of internet users grows, the usefulness of the Internet grows, too, at a much higher rate.

Wall Street Wants to Join In the Web-Based Businesses

Wall Street firms took notice of the immense growth of these new internet-based companies, and capital began to pour into these new start-ups. The reason they did not fear the risk was that Jeff Bezos’s view started to expand among stockbrokers. Jeff Bezos stated that profit can be sacrificed temporarily to reach rapid growth in the market. Shortly, this strategy was called: Get big fast! This blind belief in growth led to the doubling of Amazon.com’s stock price in just a couple of weeks; other web-based companies enjoyed similar benefits in the stock market.

Dot-Com Bubble

The easily accessible capital started to attract more and more entrepreneurs; a lot of new start-ups popped up without any solid business plan or experience in this industry. After a while, stockbrokers and financial analysts noticed that there is no real revenue behind these new e-commerce businesses, but they did not stop recommending these stocks (IPOs) to their customers because they did not want to risk their incoming earnings. Venture capitalists also knew that most of these companies will not thrive, but they were blinded by the possibility to find something similar like Amazon.com or eBay.com. Finally, the stock market became so overwhelmed that the Federal Reserve Bank needed to raise the federal reserve interest rate in early 2000 to stop this bubble grow further. Shortly after that, the trust in “Get big fast” dissolved, and the stock market collapsed; the NASDAQ lost more than two-thirds of its value.

Benefits of the Dot-Com Bubble

After the bubble popped, the possibilities in using the Web did not disappear at all. E-commerce companies like Amazon.com and eBay.com, which prioritized customer experience, survived and turned out to be hugely profitable worldwide. In fact, providing good customer experience became the widespread view among e-commerce companies. Furthermore, the billions of dollars of funds were also spent on a good cause during the dot-com bubble; the fiber-optic Internet infrastructure was built all around the USA. Without the dot-com boom, we could have not accessed fast internet speed in such a short time.
