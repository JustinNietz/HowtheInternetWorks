Glossary

The Invention of the Internet

ARPA – Later this agency broke up into what we know as NASA.
IMPs – Interface Messaging Processors that would be connected to computers to form the first internet network.
Packet switching – Created in case the telephone line connection was cut off, there would still be a way to communicate.
Queuing theory – The way data would be transmitted. Packages of data would queue, or line up at nodes of communication networks as they transited through.
TCP/IP protocol – A set of rules or procedures that computer networks would have to follow to allow them to pass their message back and forth.

The Creation of the World Wide Web

Antitrust laws – Laws in the US that regulate the conduct and organization of business corporations and are generally intended to promote competition for the benefit of consumers.
Internet Explorer – Microsoft’s take on the web browser that took elements of Netscape’s browser, but made it free.
Mosaic – This is Mark Andreessen’s first graphical web browser. It added features to the web that no one had before and made the browser look more graphically pleasing.
Netscape – It is Mark Andreessen and Jim Clark’s company based off Mosaic. They created a new browser called Navigator that was sold.
Software platform – This is the software which other programs run on top.

The Search Engine

Angel investors – These investors give support to start-ups at the initial moments and when most investors are not prepared to back them.
IPO – Initial public offering or stock market launch is a type of public offering in which shares of a company are sold to institutional investors and usually also retail investors.

Age of E-Commerce

E-commerce – E-commerce (electronic commerce) is the activity of electronically buying or selling of products on online services or over the Internet.
Federal reserve interest rate – In the United States, the federal funds rate is the interest rate at which depository institutions (banks and credit unions) lend reserve balances to other depository institutions overnight on an uncollateralized basis.
Fiber-optic Internet – It is a method of transmitting information from one place to another by sending pulses of infrared light through an optical fiber. The light is a form of carrier wave that is modulated to carry information.
Integrated circuits – An integrated circuit or also referred to as a chip, or a microchip is a set of electronic circuits on one small flat piece of semiconductor material that is normally silicon.

Communication Uprising

Digital goods – Digital goods or e-goods are intangible goods that exist in digital form. For example: e-books, downloadable music, streaming media.
Digital rights – Digital rights are those human rights and legal rights that allow individuals to access, use, create, and publish digital media or to access and use computers, other electronic devices, and telecommunications networks.
MP3 – Formally MPEG-1 Audio Layer III is a coding format for digital audio. It allows a large reduction in file sizes compared to uncompressed audio.
Social graphs – The social graphs are graphs that represent social relations between entities. In short, it is a model or representation of a social network, where the word graph has been taken from graph theory.
Web 2.0 – Web 2.0 refers to websites that emphasize user-generated content, ease of use, participatory culture, and interoperability for end users.
